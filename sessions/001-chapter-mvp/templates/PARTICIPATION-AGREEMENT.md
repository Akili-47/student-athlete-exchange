## 1. Ownership of Work Product

No institution — university, partner organization, or Exchange chapter — claims ownership of the work product produced by the athlete in an Exchange session.

The portfolio entries, Wardley Maps, and ETU session records produced during an Exchange session belong to the athlete who produced them. The athlete retains the right to use these artifacts in their professional portfolio, share them with prospective employers, and publish them publicly.

## 2. Public Verifiability

The Exchange Protocol operates on public verifiability. The ETU session records, which include the athlete's name and the nature of the strategic analysis performed, will be committed to a public version-controlled repository (e.g., GitHub).

By signing this agreement, the athlete consents to their name and session contributions being included in the public repository for the purpose of professional verification.

## 3. Confidentiality of Partner Data

While the resulting Wardley Map and ETU record are public artifacts, the partner organization may share proprietary or confidential information during the session to provide operational context.

The athlete agrees not to disclose any information explicitly marked as confidential by the partner organization or the TAC outside of the session. The public artifacts will focus on the strategic structure and the assumption delta, not on proprietary metrics or internal data.

## 4. Professional Conduct

The Exchange is a professional environment. The athlete agrees to engage with the TAC, the partner organization, and their peers with the discipline and respect expected in a professional setting. The TAC's role is to challenge; the athlete's role is to defend their reasoning and accept correction based on operational reality.
