## 2. Challenger Role

The TAC acknowledges that their role in an Exchange session is to challenge the athletes' assumptions and placements against operational reality. The TAC is not a facilitator, a teacher, or a career coach.

The TAC commits to:
- Reviewing the pre-session canvas (named user, domain boundary, known assumption traps)
- Challenging every component placement in Phase 4 (Evolution Positioning)
- Co-signing the ETU session record at the close of the session

## 3. Professional Conduct

The TAC agrees to engage with the athletes and the Map Lead with the discipline and respect expected in a professional setting. The TAC's challenge must be constructive, aimed at correcting assumptions and revealing the gap between research and reality.

The TAC will not use the session to solicit the athletes for employment, services, or personal gain outside the scope of the Exchange Protocol.
