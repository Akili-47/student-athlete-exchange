# ARC-000-PRIN-v1.0 — The Exchange Protocol: Architecture Principles

**Status:** Approved  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Owner:** Akili King

> **In plain language.** The six non-negotiable rules every Exchange chapter follows. If a chapter cannot meet all six, it is not an Exchange chapter — no matter who runs it. These principles exist so the protocol scales without requiring the founder in the room.

## Purpose

These six principles govern every Exchange chapter.
They are not guidelines. They are architecture.
A chapter that cannot demonstrate compliance with
all six principles is not an Exchange chapter.
No exception. No waiver. No founder approval required.

The principles enforce themselves through the protocol.

---

## PRINCIPLE 1 — User need anchors everything

Every session begins with a named user and a named need.
Every component on every map traces to that user need.
If a component cannot be traced to the user need,
it does not belong on the map.

**Derived from:** Wardley Mapping doctrine.  
**Enforced by:** TAC challenge during Phase 3 (Value Chain).  
**Compliance check:** Every map submission includes
a named user at the top of the value chain.

---

## PRINCIPLE 2 — Domain expertise governs quality

No map is accepted without TAC challenge.
The TAC must have operated in the mapped domain
at a professional level within the last five years.
Academic knowledge of a domain does not qualify.
The TAC evaluates maps against what actually happens
in their domain — not against published descriptions.

**Derived from:** West Point TAC model.  
**Enforced by:** TAC qualification criteria (see ARC-000-FRMW).  
**Compliance check:** TAC credential documented
before first session. Renewed every five years.

---

## PRINCIPLE 3 — The practitioner owns the work

Portfolio entries are the property of the athlete
who produced them. No institution — university,
partner organization, or Exchange chapter —
can claim ownership of athlete work product.

The dual-hash Work Ledger enforces this boundary
mathematically. Institutional policy cannot
override a cryptographic ownership record.

**Derived from:** Dual-hash Work Ledger design.  
**Enforced by:** Private payload architecture.
Athlete holds the key. No one else does.  
**Compliance check:** Every portfolio entry
dual-hashed and timestamped before session closes.

---

## PRINCIPLE 4 — The assumption delta is the value

AI-generated and online-research-derived component
placements must be challenged by a domain expert.
The gap between where research places a component
and where a domain expert places it — based on
operational reality — is the strategic value
the athlete captures.

This delta is ungoogleable. It cannot be
produced by AI alone. It requires a human
who has operated in the domain.

**Derived from:** HSBI gap doctrine.  
**Enforced by:** FR-005 (evolution positioning
challenged against actual landscape).  
**Compliance check:** ETU at session close
documents the top assumption corrections
and their source.

---

## PRINCIPLE 5 — The protocol governs, not the founder

Any chapter that meets these six principles
is an Exchange chapter. No permission is required
from the founder. No approval process exists.
The protocol is the authority.

This principle exists to prevent founder dependency
from becoming a scaling constraint. The Exchange
must operate without Akili King in the room.

**Derived from:** Mission Command doctrine.  
**Enforced by:** Public GitHub repository.
The protocol lives here. Anyone can read it.
Anyone can verify compliance. No founder required.  
**Compliance check:** Chapter reads and signs
compliance checklist (see ARC-000-FRMW).

---

## PRINCIPLE 6 — Open method, proprietary substrate

Wardley Mapping is Creative Commons licensed.
The session format is MIT licensed.
The ECHO execution intelligence substrate is proprietary.

The method is free. Anyone can use it.
The dataset that compounds through every session
is not free. It is the moat.

This is the open approaches gameplay:
drop the barrier to adoption at the visible layer,
protect the substrate that competitors
cannot replicate without equivalent deployment time.

**Derived from:** Open approaches Wardley gameplay.  
**Enforced by:** License structure.
CC for method. MIT for protocol. Proprietary for substrate.  
**Compliance check:** No chapter agreement
grants rights to ECHO substrate data.
