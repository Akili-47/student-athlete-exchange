## What this is

A one-page briefing. Read it before your first session.
Fifteen minutes. No quiz. No follow-up.

---

## What the Exchange is

The Exchange is a program that helps you build
professional credibility while you are still competing.

You sit down with someone who has operated at a high
level in an industry you care about. Over a few hours,
you map how that industry actually works — not how it is
described online or in class, but how value actually
moves and where the real leverage is.

You leave each session with a map. The map goes into
your portfolio. Over four years, that portfolio grows
into something nobody else your age has: evidence that
you can think strategically about a named industry,
with your work reviewed by someone who lives in it.

---

## What you commit to

- **Show up ready to be wrong.** Research gets some
  things right and some things wrong. You will not
  know which until a domain expert challenges your
  assumptions. That challenge is the point.
- **Do the work.** There is no test. There is no grade.
  There is a session, and you either produce a map
  or you don't.
- **Own your record.** Your portfolio is yours — not
  your university's, not your coach's, not the Exchange's.

---

## What you get back

- A portfolio of maps — eight to sixteen of them by
  the time you graduate. Each signed by the domain
  expert who challenged you on it.
- A professional reference network. The people who
  challenge your maps are people who can hire you,
  refer you, or vouch for you later.
- A record you control. Your portfolio is stored
  in a way that cannot be taken from you.

---

## Who the TAC is

TAC stands for Tactical Advising Coach.

The TAC is someone who has operated professionally
in the industry you are mapping, within the last
five years. Not a professor. Not a motivational
speaker. Someone who has actually done the work.

The TAC's job is not to teach you. The TAC's job is
to challenge what you got wrong — so that what ends
up in your portfolio is right.

You are expected to push back. If you disagree with
a correction, say so. The TAC expects it. The point
of the session is the conversation, and a one-sided
conversation isn't one.

---

## What the Work Ledger is

Every map you produce gets a record attached to it.
That record proves two things:

1. You made it, at a specific date and time.
2. Nobody can alter it later — not the Exchange,
   not your university, not anyone.

The record lives in a system that does not depend on
any single institution staying in business. If every
one of them disappeared tomorrow, your record would
still be there, verifiable by anyone.

Your portfolio is your property. Property needs proof
of ownership. The Work Ledger is the proof.

---

## The four-year arc

You evolve across four years.

Session 1, you are learning the tool. By the time you
have sixteen sessions behind you, you are producing
maps that experts pay attention to. Graduation does
not end the arc — you leave with a portfolio, a method,
and a network that all continue to compound.

Your athletic career is the starting phase of a
professional career, not the prelude to one. The
Exchange is built around that fact.

---

## What the Exchange is not

- **Not a scholarship.** You receive no money
  through the Exchange.
- **Not a job placement service.** The Exchange does
  not get you hired. It produces evidence that makes
  it easier for you to get yourself hired.
- **Not an NIL deal.** There is no compensation
  attached. No name, image, or likeness rights are
  exchanged.

If any of those are what you are looking for, this is
the wrong program. Say so — we will point you to the
right one.

---

## Three things to do before your first session

1. **Read the Wardley Mapping 101 guide.** Fifteen
   minutes. Plain language. Built by the community.  
   https://www.wardleymaps.com/guides/wardley-mapping-101

2. **Write down three things you believe to be true
   about the industry you are about to map.** You are
   almost certainly wrong about at least one of them.
   Finding out which is the value.

3. **Bring those three assumptions to the session
   ready to defend or revise them.** The TAC will
   challenge them. Do not take it personally.
   That is the session working.
