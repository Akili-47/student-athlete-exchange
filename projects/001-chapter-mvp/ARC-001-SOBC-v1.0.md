## CASE 1 — STRATEGIC CASE

### The problem

MEAC student athletes graduate with athletic credentials
and academic transcripts. Neither document answers
the question an employer actually asks:

*Can this person think strategically about my industry?*

NIL programs address income during enrollment.
Career services addresses resume formatting.
Life skills programs address personal development.

None of them produce verifiable evidence of
professional-grade strategic thinking.

The gap is not motivation. MEAC athletes are
among the most disciplined, high-performing
people on any campus. The gap is infrastructure.
No protocol exists to capture and document
the strategic work they are capable of producing.

### The House v. NCAA context

The House v. NCAA settlement creates a $20.5 billion
compliance obligation across Division I athletics.
MEAC institutions face the same mandate as Power Five
programs with a fraction of the compliance budget.

Every compliance investment must do more than one job.

The Exchange satisfies the beyond-NIL professional
development mandate while producing auditable records
that satisfy accreditation requirements — at near-zero
operational cost.

### The strategic position

The Exchange occupies uncontested space.

University career services operates in commodity space —
resume reviews, job boards, interview prep.
NIL compliance tools operate in product space —
disclosure tracking, marketplace management.
Leadership and life skills programs operate in
product space — workshops, speakers, certifications.

None of them produce domain-specific strategic analysis
validated by practitioners who have operated at the
highest levels of their fields.

That space is genesis. Unoccupied. The Exchange
is there first.

### Origin and validation

The Exchange Protocol originates from fourteen years
of documented development:

- 2012: Foot in the Door — internship matching platform,
  Portland, OR. Portfolio-as-credential concept first identified.
- 2013: Student Athlete Exchange (SAX) — first place,
  OSU Startup Weekend. Published in Athletic Management.
  University FTE replacement model identified.
- 2024: GP Monticello field deployment — seven months,
  3,000 pages of structured execution intelligence,
  $1.8M in identified cost savings. Protocol validated
  in live operations.
- 2026: The Exchange Protocol — MEAC MVP deployment.
  ECHO execution intelligence substrate underneath.

## CASE 2 — ECONOMIC CASE

### Cost of the current state

A university career services FTE costs $45,000–65,000
annually in salary alone, before benefits, overhead,
and management cost. Outcomes are measured by
placement rates — a lagging indicator that tells you
what happened, not what the athlete can do.

Employer surveys consistently show that athletic
credentials and academic transcripts do not answer
the questions that matter in a hiring decision.
The matching problem identified in 2012 remains unsolved.

### Benefit of The Exchange

One Exchange chapter produces:

- A portfolio entry per athlete per session —
  inspectable, permanent, employer-facing evidence
  of strategic thinking in a named domain
- A Wardley Map deliverable per partner organization —
  competitive intelligence produced at below-market cost
- A TAC evaluation record — the most credible
  professional reference an athlete has at graduation,
  based on four years of documented work product
- An ETU dataset — structured execution intelligence
  that compounds in value with every session

These outputs replace what a career services FTE
produces, at a fraction of the cost, with outputs
that are verifiable rather than self-reported.

### Measurable outcomes

| Metric | Current state | Exchange target |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio entries at graduation | 0 | 8–16 per athlete |
| Employer response rate to portfolio | N/A | Measurable by session 2 |
| TAC-to-hire pipeline | None | 1+ per cohort per year |
| Partner org engagement | Ad hoc | 1 per semester minimum |
| Career services cost | $45K–65K FTE | $0–4K protocol cost |

## CASE 3 — COMMERCIAL CASE

### Revenue streams

**Stream 1: University contract**
The Exchange replaces or supplements one career
services FTE function. Contract value: $15,000–35,000
annually per chapter. Offset against FTE cost,
the university saves $10,000–50,000 per year
while producing better outcomes.

**Stream 2: Partner organization engagement fee**
Each partner organization pays an engagement fee
to receive a Wardley Map deliverable produced by
athletes under TAC supervision. Fee: $2,500–7,500
per engagement. One engagement per semester
at MVP stage.

**Stream 3: TAC certification network**
As the protocol scales, TAC certification becomes
a revenue stream. TACs pay a certification fee
to be listed in the Exchange network and gain
access to the pre-vetted talent pipeline.
Fee: $500–1,500 per TAC per year at scale.

The structured execution intelligence dataset
generated through every Exchange session becomes
a proprietary training substrate for AI systems
operating in sports business, NIL, and adjacent domains.
