> **In plain language.** How to talk to the people who can say yes or no to The Exchange. This document breaks down the specific opening argument, the likely objections, and the escalation path for each of the six primary stakeholders mapped in `ARC-001-STKE-v2.0.md`. 

## Purpose

A stakeholder analysis tells you who is in the room. An engagement strategy tells you what to say to them.

This document operationalizes the stakeholder analysis into a playbook for deployment. It provides the specific framing required to move each actor from orientation to action, minimizing friction and anticipating objections before they are raised.

---

## 1. The Athletic Director (The Deployment Authority)

**The context:** Managing a shrinking budget under House v. NCAA settlement pressure. Needs auditable programming but cannot afford new headcount.
**The goal:** Approval to run the MVP session.

### The Opening Argument
"The House settlement mandates beyond-NIL professional development, but every dollar you spend on compliance is a dollar you cannot spend on the field. The Exchange satisfies the professional development mandate and produces auditable records for SACSCOC accreditation — at zero marginal cost to your department. It replaces a career services function without requiring a contract to begin."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "We already have a leadership program."
- **Response:** "Leadership programs produce stories. The Exchange produces a verifiable artifact — a map of a real industry, co-signed by a practitioner — that proves the athlete can read a domain they have never worked in. Employers hire for judgment, not stories."
- **Objection:** "I don't have the staff to run this."
- **Response:** "It requires zero FTEs. The protocol runs itself. The Map Lead runs the session, the TAC provides the expertise, and the output is committed to a public repository."

### Escalation Path
If the AD hesitates on compliance grounds, immediately bring in the Compliance Officer and pivot to the regulatory argument (Bylaw 16.3).

## 2. The Compliance Officer (The Regulatory Veto)

**The context:** The risk manager. Fears Bylaw 16 violations and misclassified NIL activity. Needs absolute clarity on boundaries.
**The goal:** Clearance that the protocol does not violate NCAA amateurism or extra benefit rules.

### The Opening Argument
"The Exchange is classified strictly under NCAA Bylaw 16.3 as permissible career counseling and professional development. It is not an NIL inducement. Every session is co-signed, version-controlled, and publicly auditable. There is no liability in the documentation because the documentation is the product."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "Is the Wardley Map considered an athletic service or NIL deliverable?"
- **Response:** "No. It is an academic and professional work product. The Athlete Participation Agreement explicitly separates this from athletic performance, satisfying Bylaw 12."
- **Objection:** "Are the TACs providing impermissible extra benefits?"
- **Response:** "No. TACs are providing career counseling and life skills programming, which is explicitly mandated by Bylaw 16.3.1.2. They provide no financial compensation or preferential treatment."

### Escalation Path
Provide the SOBC (`ARC-001-SOBC-v1.1.md`) and the Stakeholder Analysis (`ARC-001-STKE-v2.0.md`) directly to Institutional Legal Counsel for review.

## 3. The Employer (The End User)

**The context:** Hiring managers drowning in generic resumes and AI-generated cover letters. Needs a high-fidelity signal of judgment.
**The goal:** Acceptance of the Wardley Map portfolio as a valid, differentiated credential.

### The Opening Argument
"Every resume tells a story, and every candidate claims to be a strategic thinker. The athletes coming out of The Exchange don't tell stories. They bring the board. They will hand you a documented record of how they mapped a real industry, corrected by a veteran operator, under conditions that cannot be faked. You will be looking at actual judgment, not claimed potential."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "I don't know how to read a Wardley Map."
- **Response:** "You don't need to be an expert in the framework. The map is just the artifact. What you are evaluating is the athlete's ability to explain *why* they placed components where they did, and how they responded when the TAC challenged their assumptions."
- **Objection:** "How do I know the TAC didn't just do the work for them?"
- **Response:** "The ETU session record captures the specific assumption corrections. You can see exactly what the athlete got wrong and how they adjusted. The process is transparent."

### Escalation Path
Offer to let the employer serve as a TAC for one session so they can see the rigor of the challenge process firsthand.

## 4. The TAC (The Domain Expert)

**The context:** A busy professional who wants to give back but hates unstructured mentorship and wasted time.
**The goal:** Commitment to serve as the challenger for one session.

### The Opening Argument
"We need your operational reality, not a motivational speech. The athletes will build a map of your industry before you arrive. Your job is to walk in, look at their map, and tell them where their assumptions are wrong based on what actually happens in the room. Two hours maximum. Defined role. You leave with a documented record of the talent you just evaluated."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "I don't have time to prepare a presentation."
- **Response:** "You prepare nothing. The athletes do the pre-work. You just bring your 10+ years of domain currency and react to what they built."
- **Objection:** "What if they don't know anything about my industry?"
- **Response:** "That is the point. They are practicing being the person who does not know the room. Your corrections are the training mechanism."

### Escalation Path
If time is the absolute barrier, ask for a 30-minute asynchronous review of a completed map rather than a live session.

## 5. The Partner Organization

**The context:** A firm operating in the target domain. Needs strategic intelligence and talent.
**The goal:** Agreement to serve as the subject of a mapping session.

### The Opening Argument
"See your industry mapped from the athlete's perspective, corrected by a veteran operator. Getting a landscape map built by high-performing athletes and challenged by a practitioner is intelligence no consulting firm produces. You get the map, and you get early access to the talent that built it."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "We can't share proprietary information."
- **Response:** "You don't. The mapping is done using open-source intelligence and the TAC's general domain knowledge. You are the subject of the map, not the source of the data."
- **Objection:** "Why should we trust a strategy map built by college students?"
- **Response:** "You shouldn't trust the first draft. You trust the final artifact because it has passed through the TAC quality gate — an operator with a decade of experience in your field."

### Escalation Path
Provide a sanitized example of a previous Wardley Map and ETU record to demonstrate the quality of the output.

---

## 6. The MEAC Student Athlete

**The context:** High-performance individual with zero free time. Needs a post-sport advantage but cannot take on more friction.
**The goal:** Enrollment and commitment to the pre-work.

### The Opening Argument
"The evaluation system is structurally incapable of seeing what you actually build on the field. When you graduate, your athletic identity ends. The Exchange gives you the board before the game starts. You will practice being the person who does not know the room, and you will leave with a co-signed record showing you figured it out anyway. You will be the only person in the hiring pool who can show their reasoning — not just claim it."

### Likely Objections & Handling
- **Objection:** "I don't have time for another class."
- **Response:** "This is not a class. It is a session. The pre-work is focused, the session is time-boxed, and the ETU is completed in the last ten minutes. It is designed specifically for your schedule."
- **Objection:** "I'm focusing on going pro / maximizing my NIL right now."
- **Response:** "NIL income is temporary. A portfolio is permanent. Even if you go pro, you will eventually transition. Build the portfolio now while you have access to the TACs."

### Escalation Path
Connect the athlete with a peer who has completed a session and used the portfolio in an interview.
