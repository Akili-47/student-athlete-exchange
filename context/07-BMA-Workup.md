# Source Document: BMA Workup (SAX Workup 12.1.21)
**Context:** Student Athlete Xchange General Overview and Workup
**Relevance to SAX:** The operational model, curriculum, and core mission

## Core Thesis
The BMA Workup provides the clearest statement of the SAX mission: "No mechanism exists that effectively accounts for the NCAA Athlete's current lifestyle and provides a foundational framework that allows the Athlete to become an Effective Executive."

## Key Mechanisms

### The Curriculum
The curriculum is built on Peter Drucker's *Managing Oneself* as Week 1, followed by Business Model Generation, Lean Startup methodology, and Agile principles. This is a rigorous, established business curriculum applied directly to the athlete's context.

### The TAC (Tactical Advising Coach)
The TAC is not a mentor or a facilitator. The TAC is a domain expert who challenges the athlete's assumptions. The TAC must have operated in the domain being mapped within the last five years to provide valid, operational reality checks.

### The Underlying Principles
The foundation is built on the Army Leader Development Model, the BizModelAthlete Lean Canvas, the "Know Make Do" framework, and the Individual Development Plan guide. These sources shaped the six non-negotiable principles in the SAX governance architecture.

### The Competitive Landscape
The workup includes a detailed analysis of competitors (INFLCR, Game Plan, Athlete Network, Praxis, Degreed), including funding, tech stack, and news data.

## Application to The Exchange Protocol
The BMA Workup is the **evidence base** for the claims in the SAX GitHub repository. It demonstrates the evolution of the model from a theoretical concept to a structured curriculum with a defined operational role (the TAC).

The curriculum and the TAC model are central to the **Architecture Principles (ARC-000-PRIN)**. The competitive analysis feeds directly into the **Risk Register (ARC-001-RISK)** and the **Business Case (ARC-001-SOBC)**.
