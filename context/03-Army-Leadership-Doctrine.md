# Source Document: British Army Leadership Doctrine (2021)
**Context:** British Army Leadership Doctrine (AC 72029)
**Relevance to SAX:** Mission Command and the governance philosophy

## Core Thesis
The British Army Leadership Doctrine (AC 72029) codifies leadership development and the philosophy of Mission Command. The CGS foreword states: "Doctrine is indispensable to an army... it provides a common philosophy, a common language, a common purpose, and a unity of effort" [1].

## Key Mechanisms

### Mission Command
Mission Command is the central philosophy of command and leadership in the British Army. It relies on a clear understanding of the commander's intent, the empowerment of subordinates to act independently within that intent, and mutual trust [1].

### Leadership Development
The doctrine emphasizes continuous leadership development, recognizing that leadership is a learned skill that requires practice, reflection, and feedback [1].

### The Audit Trail
The British Army maintains a rigorous audit trail for all decisions and actions. This ensures accountability, facilitates learning from experience, and provides a verifiable record of performance [1].

## Application to The Exchange Protocol
The British Army Leadership Doctrine provides the governance philosophy for the Exchange Protocol.

The principle of **Mission Command** is reflected in the decentralized structure of the Exchange, where individual chapters operate autonomously within the overarching principles of the protocol.

The emphasis on **continuous development** and the **audit trail** directly informs the design of the **Execution Trace Unit (ETU)** and the **Work Ledger**. These mechanisms ensure that every session is documented, verified, and added to a permanent, third-party-readable record.

The doctrine's argument for a "common philosophy, a common language, a common purpose, and a unity of effort" is the precise justification for the **ArcKit governance architecture**. The governance documents (Principles, Requirements, Risk Register, etc.) provide the doctrine for the Exchange Protocol.
