# Context: Academic Foundations — SDT, SA, and Followership

**Source Material:** 
- Uhl-Bien et al. (2014) "Followership Theory: A Review and Research Agenda"

- Endsley & Jones (2004) "Designing for Situation Awareness"
- Ryan & Vansteenkiste "Self-Determination Theory: Metatheory, Methods, and Meaning"
- Van den Broeck & Slemp "Leadership: A Self-Determination Theory Perspective"
- "Personal Application: Leading Yourself" (Synthesized Application)
- "The Future Is Now" (Athletic Management, 2013)

## Purpose in ArcKit
These documents provide the peer-reviewed empirical evidence base for the Exchange Protocol's psychological and operational mechanisms. They transition the protocol from relying solely on military doctrine and practitioner frameworks to being grounded in rigorous academic research.

## Key Extractions for Governance Generation

### 1. Followership as Co-Production (Uhl-Bien et al., 2014)
*   **Concept:** Followership is not passive obedience; it is an active, dynamic process that co-constructs leadership outcomes. Leadership only occurs if there is followership ("allowing oneself to be influenced").
*   **Protocol Mapping:** Validates the TAC role design. The TAC is not a leader imposing direction; the athlete-TAC relationship is a co-production of the learning outcome captured in the Execution Trace Unit (ETU).

### 2. Authentic Followership and SDT (Leroy et al., 2015)
*   **Concept:** Authentic followership (enacting one's true self at work) interacts with authentic leadership to satisfy basic psychological needs (competence, autonomy, relatedness).
*   **Protocol Mapping:** The Exchange Protocol's requirement for athletes to engage in Self Mapping (Drucker) and maintain a Work Ledger forces authentic functioning, leading to higher autonomous work motivation.

### 3. Self-Determination Theory (SDT) (Ryan, Vansteenkiste, Van den Broeck)
*   **Concept:** SDT is a macro-theory of human motivation asserting that people have an inherent tendency to grow and integrate experiences, provided their basic needs for autonomy, competence, and relatedness are met.
*   **Protocol Mapping:** The protocol is designed as an environment that satisfies these needs. The decentralized, chapter-based structure provides autonomy; the project-based execution builds competence; the TAC relationship provides relatedness.

### 4. Situation Awareness (SA) (Endsley & Jones, 2004)
*   **Concept:** SA is the perception of elements in the environment (Level 1), the comprehension of their meaning (Level 2), and the projection of their status in the near future (Level 3). User-centered design must organize technology around the way users process information to prevent "SA Demons" like data overload.
*   **Protocol Mapping:** The ETU and Work Ledger are SA mechanisms. They force the athlete to perceive, comprehend, and project their career trajectory, preventing the "out-of-the-loop syndrome" common in traditional, passive athlete development programs.

### 5. Personal Application: Leading Yourself
*   **Concept:** Applying Boyd's Destruction and Creation, the OODA Loop, and Mission Command to personal development. It requires continuous Execution, Capture, Honing, and Optimization.
*   **Protocol Mapping:** This is the operational engine of the athlete's daily routine within the Exchange. The protocol forces the "Capture" phase (which most people skip) through mandatory documentation, enabling the "Hone" and "Optimize" phases.

### 6. The True Origin (The Future Is Now, 2013)
*   **Concept:** The original article detailing the OSU Startup Weekend, written by Akili King. It explicitly identifies the gap between athletics and academics and uses the 54-hour immersion to bridge it.
*   **Protocol Mapping:** Provides the documentary evidence for the 2013 proof of concept, which was itself the culmination of the realization that began with the 1997 NFL cut.
