# Source Document: Level Launch (2016)
**Context:** "The experts in salesletics" transition program
**Relevance to SAX:** The multi-stakeholder journey structure and the athletic identity crisis

## Core Thesis
Level Launch was a structured transition and sales training program for college athletes. It recognized that the end of an athletic career triggers a profound psychological crisis—the loss of "athletic identity." The program aimed to guide athletes through this transition into successful sales careers.

## Key Mechanisms

### The Five-Phase Curriculum
1. **Retire:** Addressing the psychological crisis of losing athletic identity.
2. **Transition:** Mapping existing support systems and identifying gaps (using Schlossberg's Assessment).
3. **Develop:** A "Mini Sales MBA" covering prospecting, CRM tools, and sales finance.
4. **Attack:** Personal branding, resume building, and interview preparation.
5. **Start:** Focus on stamina, self-evaluation, and adapting to change in the first 90 days of a sales role.

### The Multi-Stakeholder Walk Documents
Level Launch designed separate "Walk" documents for each key stakeholder:
* **Athlete Walk**
* **University Walk**
* **Recruiter Walk**
* **Admin Walk**
* **Platform Walk**

## Application to The Exchange Protocol
Level Launch provides two critical elements to the SAX architecture:

1. **The Athletic Identity Crisis:** The Retire and Transition phases articulate the profound human problem the Exchange Protocol exists to solve. This psychological reality must be explicitly addressed in the **Architecture Principles (ARC-000-PRIN)** and the public framing.

2. **The Multi-Stakeholder Structure:** The five distinct Walk documents are the template for the SAX user journey documentation. The SAX MVP requires the exact same structure: Athlete (customer), University (institutional partner), Recruiter (business partner), and Admin (TAC).

The failure of Level Launch (the Walk documents were empty) is the argument for **ArcKit**: operational documentation must be mandatory and verifiable before the next phase begins.
